I attended GNOME Asia Summit 2015 at University of Indonesia, Depok. Never planned to attend this event until Pak Tuan (Estu) ask Siska if I want to join as Local Committee, suddenly I replied "Yes, of course" :D . This is my first time I attended as a volunteer for Local Committee, usually I attended an event as a participant, or just enter the event without registering (I did this thing a lot).

I went to Depok at May 5 2015 at night, then arrived in the morning at University of Indonesia Commuter Station (Thanks to Hadi who take us from the station to guest house).

At the Day -1, we met with Julita, a girl from Peru (imagine how long she take a flight to Indonesia) and Akshai from India, nothing much to do, just walking around Depok and did preparation meeting.

Day 0 - Workshop Day

Today is a workshop day, four session held at Fasilkom Universitas Indonesia (Faculty of Computer Science University of Indonesia) May 7 2015, at the day, I am in-charge for preparing speakers presentation, setting their laptop or their slides, even assist them to taking a video streaming :D

We have four session of workshop, first came from Andika Triwidada, he talk about Translating GNOME to Bahasa Indonesia. It's interesting because of his team effort, we have GNOME in Bahasa Indonesia (Indonesian). He ask to us to contributing at translation, it still have much to be done to achieve 100% of translation.

If you interested to join the translation project, you can visit l10n.gnome.org (if you see "Damned Lies", you come to the right place), send a mail to gnome-l10n-id@googlegroups.com, and Wiki of Translation Project.

The next session is Akshai, he gave workshop about MicroHOPE and other device with -Garuda-Indonesia-Airlines-look-alike- logo printed on it. Damn, that MicroHOPE board can become anything, from a buzzer, flasher, or a clock.

[caption id="attachment_3765" align="alignnone" width="683"] Akshai and a volunteer demonstrating how Expeyes and MicroHOPE can do. Taken by GNOME Asia Summit 2015 Local Committee -

Third session is David King's, he talk about how to code a program for GNOME, quite interesting and his slides have bunch of code :D and he gave us every detail, everything that we need to build a GNOME application.

[caption id="attachment_3766" align="alignnone" width="683"] Look at that code! David, you are awesome! Taken by GNOME Asia Summit 2015 Local Committee -

Last session for Day 0 came from Ekaterina Gerasimova and Alexandre Franke (I thought they are a couple, but they are not :D ), they invite us to contribute to GNOME project, and at the end of presentation, they ask to us to join a short group discussion about translating, documenting and marketing. I want to join for testing, but they didn't have any, so I made a new group with Andre Klapper! We are a "testing" guys!
